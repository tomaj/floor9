## Hook / a quick word
**AI made one thing genuinely fast: building.**

When we know what we want - or even just where we're heading - we now get there in days. What it didn't speed up is everything *around* the building. And that part is on us.

## 01 - Where AI is fast
The trick is simpler than it sounds: **if we know what we want, or at least where we're going, AI gets us there fast.** Boilerplate, first versions, migrations, digging through code nobody remembers - it all compresses.

But that speed has a second condition: **we own the whole chain end-to-end.** We have the source code, we control delivery, we can ship it ourselves - nothing to wait on, nobody else to ask. That's what lets clear direction go in and working software come out, in days, not quarters.

## 02 - The first brake: people and decisions
The moment the process fills up with people, the speed disappears. Aligning, discussing, negotiating across parties, deciding, approving, prioritising - **none of it gets faster because we picked a smarter model.** There's no magic that turns a quarter into a week when the thing we're waiting on is a decision, not the code.

A real example - same two months, two completely different outcomes:
- **A whole new portal:** moj.telekom.sk, full functionality, 2 people, 2 months, live for real clients.
- **One small feature in OneApp:** 2 months buys us a meeting with HQ and a ticket in Jira.

We built the entire new [moj.telekom.sk](https://moj.telekom.sk) - a whole portal, full functionality - with two people in two months. Ask for one small feature in OneApp and two months buys us a meeting with HQ and a ticket in Jira. Then maybe it's prioritised and built in another two months - hopefully right - or it isn't, and after six months I have nothing.

> Same calendar. One ships a whole portal. The other a single Jira ticket.

## 03 - The second brake: waiting for a yes
The other tax is waiting. A firewall rule, a server, a database, an access role - days each, just for someone to click approve. That used to be a line item. **With AI it's the whole project:** the three days I wait for a role are the three days in which the thing could already have been done. I'm not exaggerating - it happens now. Same with testing: build something in a day, then wait two weeks for someone to test it, and all that speed is gone.

### A real example
[**Dr. Helix**](https://dr-helix.telekomsk.prod.acp.sk.tmo/) - an agent that reviews your merge requests and validates the code. I built it in **1 hour**, talking straight to GitLab. That was it. Then I spent **2 more weeks** chasing people for firewall rules, permissions and approvals - just to make it real in our environment.

> 1 hour to build -> 2 weeks to be allowed to ship it

Try it: https://dr-helix.telekomsk.prod.acp.sk.tmo/

## The ask (call to colleagues)
Here's the uncomfortable part: **the work isn't the bottleneck anymore - our process is.** With AI our appetite can be enormous - 100 initiatives a quarter instead of 10 - but only if we know what we want, decide fast, and kill the dependencies. So let's tear down the manual steps and the approval queues, automate them, and stay fast *and* safe at the same time. The AI path exists. **Let's find it together.**
